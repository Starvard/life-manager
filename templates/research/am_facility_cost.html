Full CapEx + OpEx breakdown for a small-to-mid sized AM shop. Figures are USD, normalized to a US-based facility, 2025–2026 market. Treat every number as a planning-grade estimate ±20–30%; real quotes vary heavily by vendor, region, and process.

1. Scope & assumptions

  • Location: US metro (e.g. light-industrial park), 2025–2026 pricing.
  • Shift pattern: 1 operator shift (8×5) with machines running lights-out 24/7 where safe.
  • Utilization: target 65–75% machine utilization after ramp.
  • Depreciation: machines amortized over 5–7 years straight-line for cost-per-part math; CapEx figures below are purchase price, not depreciation.
  • Three reference shops: (A) desktop polymer bureau, (B) mid-size industrial polymer (SLS/MJF), (C) metal LPBF job shop. All three are covered side-by-side in each table.
  • Facility sizing: Shop A ~1,500 ft², Shop B ~4,000 ft², Shop C ~6,000–8,000 ft² (metal needs more footprint for powder handling, inert gas, and HPM work cell separation).

12. Per-machine all-in operating cost

Take annual OpEx (personnel + building + consumables + maintenance + software share) and divide by the number of production machines. This is the minimum hourly rate each printer must earn to break even.

Shops routinely charge 1.8–3× the break-even hourly rate to cover margin + overhead absorbed outside of production. Market rates roughly land at: prosumer FFF $8–$15/hr, industrial polymer powder-bed $120–$250/hr, metal LPBF $450–$900/hr.

14. Sources & caveats

  • Machine list prices: OEM websites and reseller quotes (EOS, SLM Solutions / Nikon, Velo3D, HP, Formlabs, Stratasys, Markforged, Bambu Lab) as of late 2024–2026 Q1. Expect ±20% variance by region and configuration.
  • Labor rates: US BLS Occupational Outlook (Machinists / Mechanical Engineers / Production Supervisors) + AMUG salary surveys 2023–2024.
  • Feedstock prices: direct OEM material catalogs (HP 3D HR PA 12, EOS PA 2200, Ti64 Grade 23 from Carpenter / AP&C / Tekna). Market prices for 316L and AlSi10Mg can drop 20–30% below OEM when using qualified 3rd-party powders.
  • Energy consumption: published machine datasheets (EOS M290 ~8.5 kW peak, HP MJF 5200 ~11 kW peak, SLS Fuse 1+ ~1.5 kW peak, FFF ~0.3 kW avg).
  • Service contract percentages: multiple OEM service quotes and industry reports (Wohlers 2024, SmartTech/AMPower 2024).
  • Facility fit-out: NFPA 652/654 compliance guidance and construction industry RSMeans cost indexes for light industrial.
  • All numbers are planning-grade. For a real business case, replace each line with a firm vendor quote before signing anything.
