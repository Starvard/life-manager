First-year leaps, regressions, and milestone watch

The dates below are approximate windows from Rowan’s March 16, 2026 birth date. Every baby varies; the useful part is seeing whether sleep, feeding, fussiness, and skills cluster around the same periods.

Weeks 4–6 · Apr 13–27

Early awareness jump

More alert time, stronger eye contact, start of social smiles, and bigger evening fussiness are common here.

  • Watch: smiles, calming to voices, longer alert windows.
  • Note: what settles him fastest.
Weeks 8–10 · May 11–25

2-month social/communication burst

Look for more cooing, reacting to sounds, tracking faces/toys, and better head lifting during tummy time.

  • Watch: coos, smiles back, hands opening, tummy-time head lift.
  • Note: best awake window length.
Weeks 12–15 · Jun 8–29

Hands, voice, and early coordination

He may study his hands, bring hands to mouth, bat at toys, and get more opinionated about boredom or overstimulation.

  • Watch: hands to mouth, toy batting, head turning toward voices.
  • Note: new sounds and what makes him laugh/chuckle.
Weeks 16–20 · Jul 6–Aug 3

4-month sleep regression zone

Sleep can get choppy as sleep cycles mature and skills accelerate. This is a good place to track naps, wake windows, false starts, and night wakes.

  • Watch: rolling attempts, pushing up on forearms, reaching, cooing back.
  • Note: bedtime routine, nap length, wakeups, feeding changes.
Weeks 23–27 · Aug 24–Sep 21

6-month skill consolidation

Expect more purposeful reaching, rolling, object exploration, stronger trunk control, and possible feed/sleep shifts.

  • Watch: rolling both ways, supported sitting, reaching/grabbing, babbling.
  • Note: solids readiness conversations with pediatrician.
Weeks 29–33 · Oct 5–Nov 2

Separation + mobility prep

More object permanence, stronger preference for caregivers, and early crawling/scooting practice can disrupt sleep and mood.

  • Watch: looking for hidden objects, stranger anxiety, pivoting/scooting.
  • Note: bedtime protests, clingy windows, comfort strategy.
Weeks 34–39 · Nov 9–Dec 14

9-month milestone window

Communication and mobility may jump: sitting, crawling, pulling toward objects, babbling strings, and games like peekaboo.

  • Watch: sitting without support, moving toward toys, responding to name.
  • Note: new consonant sounds, gestures, and favorite games.
Weeks 42–46 · Jan 4–Feb 1

Pre-walking / big opinions phase

Pulling to stand, cruising attempts, pointing/gesturing, and more obvious preferences can make routines feel wobbly again.

  • Watch: pulling to stand, pincer grasp, gestures, copying sounds.
  • Note: frustration triggers and independent play length.
Weeks 49–52 · Feb 15–Mar 16

1-year transition

First words, standing/walking attempts, imitation, games, and sleep/nap transitions can all stack up around the first birthday.

  • Watch: waves, claps, first words, standing, cruising/walking attempts.
  • Note: nap pattern, meals, big new skills, birthday questions for pediatrician.
